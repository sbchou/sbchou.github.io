Why Egg?

The Egg is a marvelous feat of natural engineering: both fragile and incredibly strong. The fact that it hatches new life never fails to amaze us. Eggs morph from simple, modular, feats of engineering to warm, fuzzy, chicks: just like our stories should.

All Science is Human

We often forget that science, too, is the study of "humanities": not the least because humans create the study of science, but moreover, human input shapes our research along every step of the way. By providing tools for simple, intuitive storytelling-- based off the universal model of the children's storybook-- we hope to remind readers through the medium that science is human.

“If there is a hard, high wall and an egg that breaks against it, no matter how right the wall or how wrong the egg, I will stand on the side of the egg. Why? Because each of us is an egg, a unique soul enclosed in a fragile egg. Each of us is confronting a high wall. The high wall is the system which forces us to do the things we would not ordinarily see fit to do as individuals . . . We are all human beings, individuals, fragile eggs. We have no hope against the wall: it's too high, too dark, too cold. To fight the wall, we must join our souls together for warmth, strength. We must not let the system control us -- create who we are. It is we who created the system.” Haruki Murakami
